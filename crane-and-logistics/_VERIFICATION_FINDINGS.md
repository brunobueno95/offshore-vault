# Crane and logistics verification findings

Key verified items from tree scoping research:

- Forklifts ARE used offshore but narrowly. Standard NCS cert bundle for kranforer, dekksbas, or materialkoordinator is T1 + T2 + T4. T3 (narrow-aisle/order-picker) is effectively absent offshore, as are T5-T8 (those live at onshore supply bases: Dusavik, Tananger, Mongstad, CCB Agotnes).
- ATEX zoning drives the electric-vs-diesel choice. Electric (lead-acid or ATEX-approved lithium) dominates stores, galley corridors, workshops. Pipe deck and process areas often require ATEX 2G or 3G converted trucks (Pyroban, Miretti).
- G4 traverskran and G20 fastmontert hydraulisk kran are unambiguously part of the NCS crane operator scope. The Havtil / Samarbeidsrådet Petroleum training matrix (Modul P-2.1, P-3.1, P-4.1, P-4.2) states that G5 offshorekran competence implicitly covers G4 Traverskran, G20 Fastmontert kran, and G7 Vinsj offshore. G20 theory (P-2.1) is a mandatory prerequisite before G5 theory.
- G4 locations offshore: drill floor manipulators, BOP shop, subsea tree bay, moonpool BOP gantries, engine rooms (one per pontoon on semi-subs), mud pump room, cement unit, HPU rooms, pump/filter rooms, mekanisk verksted, module handling hall, riser gantry. Typical inventory: 10-30 G4 units per installation.
- G20 locations offshore: moonpool man-riding knuckle-booms, cement unit handling crane, helideck refueling crane, provisions crane, wall-mounted slew cranes (e.g. 23t units on Aker BP Hugin A), flareboom service crane, stern crane. Typical inventory: 5-20 G20 units per installation.
- Offshore Norge guideline 102 is referenced as historical/user-named. Current operational lifting reference on the NCS is NORSOK R-003N. Offshore Norge 113 (fall protection) and 116 (packing, securing and transport of cargo, replacing OLF 116) are live.
